Sutta Pitaka
Saüyutta Nikàya
Division III Ý Khandhaka
Book 28 Ý Nàga Saüyutta
Chapter 1 Ý Nàga Vagga

Namo tassa bhagavato arahato sammà sambudhassa

 

28. 1. 1.

1 (1) Suddhikaü Ý Purity

1. I heard thus. At one time the Blessed One was living in the monastery offered by Anàthapiõóika in Jeta's grove in Sàvatthi.

2. The Blessed One addressed the monks from there:

3. ßMonks, these four are the serpent births. What four? Serpents born from an egg, born from a womb, serpents born in moisture, and serpents born spontaneously.û

 

28. 1. 2.

2. (2) Panitataraü Ý Most exalted

1. I heard thus. At one time the Blessed One was living in the monastery offered by Anàthapiõóika in Jeta's grove in Sàvatthi.

2. The Blessed One addressed the monks from there:

3. ßMonks, these four are serpent births.

4. ßMonks, of the serpents born from an egg; those born in a womb, born in moisture, and born spontaneously are exalted.

5. ßMonks, of the serpents born from an egg; and born in a womb, those born in moisture, and born spontaneously are exalted.

6. ßMonks, of the serpents born from an egg; born in a womb, born in moisture, those born spontaneously are exalted.

7. ßMonks, these four are serpent births.û

 

28. 1. 3.

3. (3) Uposathaü I Ý Full moon Observances I

1. I heard thus. At one time the Blessed One was living in the monastery offered by Anàthapiõóika in Jeta's grove in Sàvatthi.

2. Then a certain monk approached the Blessed One, worshipped, and sat on a side.

3. Sitting, that monk said to the Blessed One: ßVenerable sir, how is it that a certain serpent, born from an egg, observing the full moon observances, would even give up his body?û

4. ßHere, monk, to a certain serpent born from an egg it occurs: `Earlier we acted in two ways by body, speech, and mind, and after death were born with the serpents born from eggs.'

5. ß`Today we develop the right conduct by body, speech, and mind. In this manner we go to a good state, go to heaven after death.'

6. ß`Therefore at this time we develop the right conduct by body speech and mind.'

7. ßMonk, this is the reason that a certain serpent, born from an egg, observing the full moon observances, would even give up his body.û

28. 1. 7.

7. (7) Sutaü I Ý They Learn I

1. I heard thus. At one time the Blessed One was living in the monastery offered by Anàthapiõóika in Jeta's grove in Sàvatthi.

2. Then a certain monk approached the Blessed One, worshipped, and sat on a side.

3. Sitting, that monk said to the Blessed One: ßVenerable sir, how is it that a certain one, after death, is born with the serpents born from eggs?û

4. ßHere, monk, a certain one acts in two ways by body, speech, and mind. He learns that serpents born from eggs enjoy long life, beauty, and much pleasantness.

5. ßThen it occurs to him, `May I be born with the serpents born from eggs after death.

6. ßAfter the breakup of the body, after death, he is born with the serpents born from eggs.

7. ßMonk, this is the reason for a certain one to be born with the serpents born from eggs after death.û

Dànupakara I, II, III, and IV Ý By Giving Gifts I, II, III, and IV

(The next forty suttas consist of four groups of ten suttas. Each sutta describes a type of gift-giving. The group of ten suttas is repeated for the egg -born [aõóaja] , womb-born [jalàbuja], moisture-born [saüsedaja], and spontaneously-born [opapàtika].)

 

(Suttas 11. through 20: Gifts: the Egg-Born)

28. 1. 11. Annadàyaka aõóaja Ý Egg-born Donor of Eatables

1. I heard thus. At one time the Blessed One was living in the monastery offered by Anàthapiõóika in Jeta's grove in Sàvatthi.

2. Then a certain monk approached the Blessed One, worshipped, and sat on a side.

3. Sitting, that monk said to the Blessed One: ßVenerable sir, how is it that a certain one, after death, is born with the serpents born from eggs?û

4. ßHere, monk, a certain one acts in two ways by body, speech, and mind. He learns that serpents born from eggs enjoy long life, beauty, and much pleasantness.

5. ßThen it occurs to him, `May I be born with the serpents born from eggs, after death.'

6. ßHe gives eatables. At the breakup of the body, after death, he is born with the serpents born from eggs.

7. ßMonk, this is the reason for a certain one to be born with the serpents born from eggs, after death.û
